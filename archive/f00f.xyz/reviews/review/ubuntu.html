Ubuntu is probably the most just-werks distro there is, because it just werks. You pop it in and it werks. It combines everything you need - drivers and software so you don't have to spend an hour installing software.

Packages

Ubuntu is the most developed-for platform, because many cross-platform devs do not care about other distros, as Ubuntu is the most popular. This means that not only will you get all Linux's software, you'll also get packaged debs, this means all you'll have to do is double click and the software is installed.

apt, like in Debian, is still shitty, although Ubuntu does look like they're putting in an effort to make it less shitty. By making snappy. The amount of GUI updater/software management tools got annoying for me though.
